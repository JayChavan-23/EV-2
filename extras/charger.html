Charge a New Electric Bike Battery For
12 hours

Take the time to fully charge your new electric bike's battery for up to 12 hours when you first acquire it. This extended first charge guarantees that current flows across all of the cells, allowing the battery to be utilized straight out of the box.

Ensure that your E-bike Battery is Charged on a Regular Basis

With frequent usage and, thus, regular charging, the lithium chemistry batteries used on electric bikes, and actually most electric bikes, should last longer. You don't need to let your electric bike battery totally deplete for the most part. Rather, charge the battery when it has between 30% and 60% capacity remaining. However, some battery experts recommend performing a thorough drain once every 30 to 40 charges. Otherwise, stick to the 30-to-60 percent rule. To charge your electric bike battery on a regular basis, you must ride it on a regular basis, so consider this another more reason to get out and ride.

Temperature Extremes Should Be Avoided

The weather affects an electric bike battery in the same manner as it affects most electric bike users. It is preferable to have moderate temperatures. Avoid temperature extremes when riding an electric bike and when charging the battery to extend the life of the battery. The battery should be kept in a temperature range of below zero to 110 degrees Fahrenheit during a charging cycle. But why go to such lengths? It would be much better if the temperature when charging was between 60 and 70 degrees Fahrenheit.

An Electric Bike Battery Should not Be Overcharged

Don't keep your electric bike battery on the charger for lengthy periods of time – at least a few days. When you do, you might cause the battery to deplete, leaving it at around 95% capacity. The charger then gets to work, charging the battery to full capacity. A succession of poor charging cycles is created by this sequence of tiny discharges and topping up. Rather, set a timer on your phone to remind you to remove the battery from the charger.
